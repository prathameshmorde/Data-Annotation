Identify Cases Of Pneumonia In Children

Instructions

Overview

A classification system that helps to identify cases of pneumonia in children in the images we provide.

Steps

  1. Examine the image.
  2. If there is a pneumoniain the image, check the appropriate box. (Yes or No)
  3. If you are uncertain about your option then you can also select Unknown option.
  4. After you click on Yes or No there would be one more assesment to check your confidence level (Low and High). It represents how likely you think a case of pneumonia is in a given image.



    Rules Tips

    Rules:

    • Look provided images carefully.
    • Read the instructions below for more insights.

    Tips:

    • There are different visual symptoms that indicate pneumonia.
    • A normal healthy image will depict clear lungs without any areas of abnormal cloudiness. There may be structured, web-like vasculature in the lungs but otherwise that area should be clear. You will also able to see diaphragm shadow.
    • A pneumonia image may include a few things like area of cloudiness in several concentrated areas or one large area. There would be general pattern where the cloudiness will obscure the structure of the lungs, heart and diaphragm.
    • If lungs, heart and diaphragm is obscured (Cloudiness around the region) or one large area then you will select Yes with High confidence.
    • When it is hard to differentiate between web like vasculature and cloudy spots then you will select Yes with Low confidence.
    • When you see a clear diaphragm shadow and no cloudiness around heart, lung , diaphragm then you will select No with High confidence.
    • When you don't see a clear diaphragm shadow and no cloudiness around heart, lung (Clear) then you will select No with Low confidence.
    • When you don't see a clear diaphragm shadow and can't differentiate between web like vasculature and cloudy spots around lung and heart then you will select Unkonwn.
